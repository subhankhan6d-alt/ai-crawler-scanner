Frequently Asked Questions

Is CrawledByBots free?

Yes. CrawledByBots includes a free plan that lets you audit up to 5 websites per scan. Every free scan checks whether leading AI crawlers can access and understand your website by analyzing robots.txt, llms.txt, structured data, crawlability, JavaScript rendering, and overall AI readiness. Upgrade only when you need to audit larger batches of websites.

What does the AI Readiness Score measure?

The AI Readiness Score measures how well your website is prepared for AI-powered search engines and large language models. It evaluates factors such as robots.txt accessibility, llms.txt implementation, structured data, crawlable HTML content, JavaScript rendering, AI crawler permissions, and other technical SEO best practices. A higher score indicates your website is better optimized for platforms like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity.

Which AI crawlers are supported?

CrawledByBots continuously monitors the most important AI crawlers used by today's leading AI platforms. The audit includes GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, Gemini-related crawlers, AmazonBot, AppleBot, Bytespider, Meta External Agent, and many more. Our crawler database is regularly updated as new AI search platforms and LLM crawlers are released, ensuring your website is tested against the latest AI ecosystem.

Why should I check AI crawler access?

AI-powered search is changing how users discover content online. If AI crawlers cannot access or interpret your website correctly, your content may be less likely to appear in AI-generated responses. CrawledByBots helps you verify AI crawler accessibility, identify technical SEO issues, improve Generative Engine Optimization (GEO), and ensure your website is ready for platforms like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity.

How often should I run an AI crawler audit?

We recommend running an AI crawler audit whenever you update your robots.txt or llms.txt files, launch a new website, publish significant content, modify your CMS, or make technical SEO changes. Monthly audits are a good practice for most websites, helping you monitor AI readiness, detect crawlability issues early, and stay optimized for the rapidly evolving AI search landscape.
